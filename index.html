Why developers are so divided over CMS

Decoupled WordPress sounds a lot like headless WordPress, and they do share some similarities. The main difference is that headless WordPress has a WordPress-based back end that can connect with any other framework for its front-end, whereas decoupled WordPress uses WordPress for both its front and back ends, while hosting them separately. In practical terms, decoupled WordPress offers the best features of conventional and headless WordPress, but headless WordPress offers much greater publishing flexibility than any other approach.
